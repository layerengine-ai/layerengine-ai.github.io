Discovery Framework

The LayerEngine
Tech Audit

Six layers. Every organization has gaps in at least three of them. Most do not know which three until someone asks the right questions.

How this works: Most organizations are already paying for most of the solution — they just have it fragmented across tools that do not talk to each other, managed by people who are not fully trained on them. This audit maps your current state across six critical layers. By the end, you will know exactly where your system is leaking and what it would look like if it worked as one engine.
01
Lead Generation
How are you attracting new business right now — buyers, sellers, recruits, or referral partners? Most organizations are paying for tools that generate activity, not leads. This layer is about identifying what is actually working and what is just costing money.
What are you currently doing to attract new business — buyers, sellers, recruits?
What tools or platforms are you paying for right now for lead generation?
Are those tools actually producing leads, or just producing activity?
What does a qualified lead look like for your organization?
02
Follow-Up
Not every lead is ready today. Most organizations have no systematic follow-up beyond the first touch — which means they are generating leads and then losing them to whoever follows up better. This layer is about what happens after the first contact.
What happens to a lead after the first contact — who follows up and how?
Do you have automated sequences running, or is it entirely manual?
How many touches does a lead get before they fall off your radar?
What percentage of your pipeline is leads you have not spoken to in 30 or more days?
03
Transaction Management
How does a deal move from contract to close in your organization? Who is responsible for each step — and does everyone actually know that? This layer is about identifying where deals stall, fall apart, or require unnecessary manual intervention.
How does a deal move from contract to close in your organization?
Who is responsible for each step — and does everyone actually know that?
Where do deals most commonly stall or fall apart?
Are you using any automation, or is it all manual coordination?
04
Recruiting
Recruiting is just the top of a sales funnel — whether you are attracting agents to your brokerage, loan officers to your team, or clients to your service. This layer is about your outbound engine, your response rates, and where the bottleneck actually is.
How are you currently recruiting agents or loan officers to your team?
What does your outbound recruiting process look like today?
How many recruiting conversations are you having per month?
What is your biggest bottleneck — finding candidates, getting responses, or converting them?
05
Retention
Winning through people means keeping them. Most organizations lose producers in the first 90 days not because the opportunity was wrong — but because no one was paying attention. This layer is about what you are doing to keep producers engaged, producing, and loyal.
What is your average producer tenure — how long do people typically stay?
What typically causes someone to leave in the first 90 days?
What are you doing today to keep producers engaged and producing?
Do your producers have their own lead generation system, or are they on their own?
06
Onboarding
The right hand does not know what the left hand is doing — and the person at the top thinks everything is fine. Onboarding is where new hires either become producers or quietly disengage. This layer is about what happens from the day someone signs to their first transaction.
Walk me through what happens from the day someone signs to their first transaction.
Who manages the onboarding process — is it documented or tribal knowledge?
What falls through the cracks most often during onboarding?
How long does it typically take a new hire to become a producing professional?
