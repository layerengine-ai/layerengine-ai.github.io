Section 1 of 4

Your Avatar — Who You're Talking To

Before you can build an offer, you need to know exactly who it's for. The more specific you are here, the more powerful your VSL will be. A message that speaks to everyone speaks to no one.

Why this matters

Your avatar is the single person you're imagining when you record your video. Not "buyers and sellers in San Diego" — but "Sarah, a 34-year-old first-time buyer in La Jolla who's been outbid three times and is starting to wonder if she'll ever own a home."

When your VSL speaks directly to that person's specific fears and frustrations, they feel like you're reading their mind — and that's what gets them to book a call.

Worked Examples
Buyer-Focused Agent

"First-time buyers in San Diego who keep losing out to all-cash offers and don't know how to compete without overpaying."

Seller-Focused Agent

"Seniors in La Jolla who want to downsize but are overwhelmed by the idea of prepping their home for sale and don't know where to start."

Relocation Specialist

"Military families relocating to Camp Pendleton who need to buy sight-unseen and are terrified of making a $600k mistake from 2,000 miles away."

Investor-Focused Agent

"Local investors who want to add a rental property but don't have time to search the MLS every day and keep missing the deals before they're gone."

Your Unique Solution — The Offer Formula

This is the core of everything. One sentence that explains exactly who you help, what result you deliver, what frustration you eliminate, and how you do it differently than every other agent.

The Offer Formula
I help {type of client} get {desirable result} without {common frustration} by using {your unique method}.
"I help first-time buyers own a home without a big down payment using my 3-step lender-approved strategy."
"I help downsizing seniors sell their homes in 14 days without endless showings by using our Private Buyer Pool system."
"I help busy professionals buy investment properties without spending hours on the MLS using my Off-Market Deal Finder system."
What are "core pillars"?

Your pillars are the 3–4 steps inside your system — the process you walk every client through. They don't need to be complicated. Think of them as the chapters of your method.

Example: If your system is called "The La Jolla Buyer Blueprint," your pillars might be: Pillar 1: Full Underwriting Before You Search → Pillar 2: Off-Market Access → Pillar 3: The Escalation Offer Strategy. These become the slide headers in your Thesis Presentation.

Proof & Authority — Your Story and Results

People don't buy promises — they buy proof. This section gives your VSL credibility. You need two things: specific results you've delivered, and the story of why you built your system.

How to write your results (with numbers)
Too Vague

"I helped a family find a great home in a competitive market."

Specific & Powerful

"Helped the Martinez family win their offer at $15k under asking price after losing out on 4 homes — closed in 28 days."

Too Vague

"My seller got a good price for their home."

Specific & Powerful

"Sold a 3-bed in Encinitas for $47k over asking in 6 days with 9 offers — no open houses, no price reductions."

Your origin story — what to write

Your origin story is 2–3 sentences that explain why you built your specific system. It humanizes you and makes your method feel intentional, not accidental.

Template: "I built [system name] after seeing [specific problem you witnessed]. I realized [insight]. So I developed [your method] specifically to [result it delivers]."

Example: "I built the La Jolla Buyer Blueprint after watching 6 clients lose their dream homes to all-cash investors in a single month. I realized the problem wasn't their budget — it was their strategy. So I developed a system that gets buyers fully underwritten and connected to off-market inventory before anyone else sees it."

Section 4 of 4

Guarantee & Call to Action

The last 60 seconds of your VSL are the most important. This is where you remove risk and tell people exactly what to do next. One clear action — no confusion, no options.

Guarantee examples
Buyer Agent

"If you don't love the home you buy, I'll sell it for free within 12 months — no commissions, no questions."

Listing Agent

"If I don't sell your home in 30 days, I'll cut my commission in half. No fine print."

Low-Risk Option

"Our first strategy call is completely free. If you don't walk away with a clear plan, you owe me nothing."

Soft Guarantee

"Cancel anytime — no long-term contracts, no pressure. I only earn your business if I earn your trust."

Your call to action — keep it ONE thing

The biggest mistake agents make is ending their VSL with "call me, email me, DM me, or fill out the form below." Pick one. The best CTA for most agents is a calendar booking link — it filters for serious buyers/sellers and eliminates phone tag.

Examples: "Click the button below to book your free 30-minute Strategy Session" → "Download my free Home Buyer Playbook" → "Text READY to [number] to get started."

Before You Fill Out the Form

4 Tips to Get the Best Results

The quality of your VSL script depends entirely on the quality of your answers. Here's how to make yours stand out.

🎯

Be Specific, Not General

Avoid "buyers and sellers in my area." Name a specific type of person, a specific neighborhood, a specific problem. Specificity = credibility.

🔢

Use Real Numbers

Don't say "sold quickly." Say "sold in 9 days for $42k over asking." Numbers are the difference between a claim and proof.

💬

Write Like You Talk

Don't write how you think a professional should sound. Write how you actually talk to clients. The AI will clean it up — your job is to be real.

⏱️

Give Yourself 15 Minutes

Don't rush this. Block out 15 minutes, close your other tabs, and think about your best clients. This is the foundation of everything you'll build.
