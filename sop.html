Section 1

Google Form → GHL Webhook

This is the automation that converts a form submission into a GHL contact. It runs entirely on autopilot via Google Apps Script. You do not need to touch it — but you need to understand how it works so you can troubleshoot it.

How It Works

  1. Client submits the Google Form at the link above.
  2. Google Apps Script (bound to the form) fires the onFormSubmit trigger.
  3. The script collects all form answers and POSTs them as JSON to the GHL webhook URL.
  4. GHL receives the payload, creates a contact, and assigns it to the pipeline.
  5. VA receives a GHL notification and sees the new contact in the CRM.

Webhook URL

This is the live GHL webhook endpoint. Do not share it externally.

https://services.leadconnectorhq.com/hooks/s45yFpjz4mgv661CXD8n/webhook-trigger/c1b52f7c-6d58-416e-a31a-501c32c5d508

Troubleshooting

If a form submission does not appear in GHL within 5 minutes:

  1. Open the Google Form → click the three-dot menu → Script editor.
  2. Click Executions in the left sidebar to see if the trigger fired.
  3. If there is an error, check the webhook URL is still active in GHL (Automation → Workflows → LayerEngine Onboarding Webhook).
  4. If the webhook workflow is paused or deleted, re-create it and update the URL in the Apps Script.

Important: The Google Form is for Solo, Launch, and Managed tiers ONLY. Lower-tier clients (Tier 1–3) use the GHL-hosted form at layerengine.ai/onboarding. Do not send the wrong form link to the wrong tier.

Domain & DNS

Every client's funnel needs to run on their own domain, not a generic GHL URL. This section covers how to connect their GoDaddy domain to their GHL subaccount.

  1. Ask the client for their GoDaddy login credentials (or have them add you as a delegate user).
  2. In GHL subaccount → Settings → Domains → + Add Domain.
  3. Enter the domain (e.g., recruit.johnsmithrealty.com) and click Save.
  4. GHL will display a CNAME record. Copy the Name and Value.
  5. Log in to GoDaddy → DNS Management for the domain.
  6. Add a new CNAME record with the Name and Value from GHL.
  7. Wait 15–60 minutes for DNS propagation. Refresh the GHL domain settings page until it shows Verified.
  8. Assign the domain to the recruiting funnel in GHL.

DNS propagation tip: Use dnschecker.org to monitor when the CNAME record has propagated globally. Do not tell the client their site is live until it shows green in all regions.

Section 2

A2P SMS Registration

A2P (Application-to-Person) registration is required by US carriers before any automated SMS can be sent from a GHL account. Without it, texts will be blocked. This applies to all US-based clients.

What You Need from the Client

  • Legal business name (exactly as registered)
  • EIN (Employer Identification Number) — US clients only
  • Business address
  • Business website URL
  • Sample SMS message they plan to send

Registration Steps

  1. In GHL subaccount → Settings → Phone Numbers → A2P Registration.
  2. Click Register Brand and fill in the business details.
  3. Click Register Campaign — select "Mixed" use case for recruiting/real estate.
  4. Enter the sample message and opt-in language.
  5. Submit. Approval typically takes 3–7 business days.
  6. Once approved, the client's phone number will be enabled for automated SMS.

Canadian clients: A2P registration is not required in Canada, but clients still need a dedicated GHL phone number. Provision one from the Phone Numbers settings page.

VSL Script Prompt

This prompt generates a Video Sales Letter (VSL) script for the client. The VSL is a 5–8 minute video the client records and embeds on their recruiting funnel landing page. Copy this prompt, fill in the [BRACKETS], and run it in Claude or Manus AI.

You are an expert direct-response copywriter specializing in real estate recruiting. Your job is to write a compelling, authentic VSL (Video Sales Letter) script for a brokerage owner who wants to recruit producing agents. CLIENT PROFILE: - Name: [CLIENT_FIRST_NAME] [CLIENT_LAST_NAME] - Brokerage/Office Name: [BROKERAGE_NAME] - Market/City: [MARKET] - Primary value proposition: [VALUE_PROPOSITION] - Target agent avatar: [AGENT_AVATAR] - Top 3 pain points solved: [PAIN_POINT_1], [PAIN_POINT_2], [PAIN_POINT_3] - Signature offer: [SIGNATURE_OFFER] - Proof points / results: [PROOF_POINTS] - Tone preference: [TONE — e.g., "direct and confident" or "warm and conversational"] TASK: Write a 5–8 minute VSL script (approximately 750–1,200 words when spoken at a natural pace) with the following structure: HOOK (0:00–0:30): Open with a bold, pattern-interrupting statement that speaks directly to the agent's biggest frustration. Do NOT start with "Hi, my name is..." Start with the pain point. PROBLEM (0:30–1:30): Agitate the problem. Why is the agent stuck? What is the industry doing wrong? Make them feel understood. CREDIBILITY (1:30–2:30): Introduce [CLIENT_FIRST_NAME] and [BROKERAGE_NAME]. Establish authority without bragging. Use specific numbers and results. THE THESIS (2:30–4:00): Present the core argument. Why is [BROKERAGE_NAME] the platform that solves the agent's problem? Walk through the value stack. THE OFFER (4:00–5:30): Describe what joining looks like. Be specific about the first 30/60/90 days. Make it feel low-risk and high-reward. THE CLOSE (5:30–end): Clear call to action. Tell them exactly what to do next (click the button below, book a call). Create urgency without being pushy. FORMATTING REQUIREMENTS: - Write in first person as [CLIENT_FIRST_NAME] - Use short sentences and natural spoken language — this will be read on camera - Add [PAUSE] markers where the speaker should pause for emphasis - Add [SMILE] or [LEAN IN] stage direction notes where appropriate - Mark the approximate timestamp at the start of each section - End with a word count estimate The script should feel authentic, not scripted. The agent watching should feel like they are having a real conversation with [CLIENT_FIRST_NAME], not watching an ad.

Pro tip: After generating the script, read it out loud yourself. If any sentence feels unnatural to say, rewrite it. A script that sounds like the client's natural voice converts far better than polished corporate copy.

Section 5

24/7 Digital Recruiter

The 24/7 Digital Recruiter is the automated system that works while the client sleeps. It combines the Meta ad campaign, the GHL funnel, and the 30-day email + SMS nurture sequence into one continuous machine. Your job is to set it up correctly and make sure it never stops running.

How the Digital Recruiter Works

An agent sees the ad → clicks → lands on the funnel → watches the VSL → fills out the form → gets added to GHL → receives automated Day 1 email + SMS → receives follow-up sequence for 30 days → books a call when ready.

The client does not need to do anything in this sequence. The system handles it. Their only job is to show up for the strategy call when an agent books one.

Key insight: Most agents will not book a call on Day 1. The 30-day sequence is what converts them. Agents who book on Day 14, 21, or 28 are often the most serious. Do not turn off the sequence early.

Section 5

Speed to Lead SOP

Speed to lead is the single most important variable in converting inbound leads. Research shows that responding within 5 minutes increases conversion by 9x compared to responding after 30 minutes. This SOP ensures the client's system responds instantly.

Automated Speed-to-Lead Workflow

  1. Lead submits form → GHL contact created instantly.
  2. Workflow fires: immediate SMS sent from client's GHL number: "Hey [First Name], it's [CLIENT_NAME] from [BROKERAGE_NAME]. Thanks for checking us out — I'd love to connect. What's the best time for a quick 20-min call this week?"
  3. Simultaneous email sent (Email #1 from the nurture sequence).
  4. GHL notification sent to client's phone: "New lead: [Lead Name] — [Lead Phone]".
  5. If lead does not reply within 24 hours: Day 1 follow-up SMS fires automatically.

Client instruction: Tell every client to respond personally to leads who reply to the automated SMS within the first hour. The automation opens the door — the client closes it. If they are not responding to replies, the system is wasted.

Section 6

Access & Security

The LayerEngine internal pages (/university and /sop) are protected by Cloudflare Zero Trust. Only approved email addresses can access them. This section covers how to grant and revoke access, and how to troubleshoot login issues.

Cloudflare Zero Trust Access

How It Works

When a user visits layerengine.ai/university or layerengine.ai/sop, Cloudflare intercepts the request and checks if the user's email is on the approved list. If it is, they receive a one-time magic link via email. If it is not, access is denied.

Granting Access to a New Client or VA

  1. Log in to dash.cloudflare.com with the LayerEngine account.
  2. Navigate to Zero Trust → Access → Applications.
  3. Click on the LayerEngine University application (or SOP application).
  4. Click Edit → Policies.
  5. Under the "Allow" policy, add the new email address to the "Emails" list.
  6. Click Save. Access is granted immediately.

SOP page access: The /sop page should only be accessible to the owner and active VAs. Do NOT add client emails to the SOP application. Clients get access to /university only.

Revoking Access

To remove a user's access (e.g., when a client's subscription expires or a VA leaves): follow steps 1–4 above, remove the email from the "Emails" list, and click Save. Access is revoked immediately.

Training Content

Add the following content to the GHL Knowledge Base. Format each entry as a Q&A pair. The more specific the question, the more accurate the AI response will be.

How to Add Content to the Knowledge Base

  1. In GHL Agency → Settings → AI → Knowledge Base.
  2. Click + Add Document.
  3. Paste the Q&A content as plain text or upload a PDF.
  4. Click Save. The AI will index the content within a few minutes.
  5. Test the bot by asking it the questions you just added. Confirm the answers are accurate.

Priority Q&A Entries to Add First

Q: What happens after I pay?
A: Within 24 hours you will receive a welcome email with a link to a short intake form. Once you complete the form, our team will build your custom GHL system and generate your Thesis Presentation and VSL script. Total setup time is 48–72 hours. You will then receive your assets and schedule your onboarding call.
Q: How long does it take to see results from the ads?
A: Most clients see their first leads within 3–7 days of launching their Meta campaign. However, the algorithm needs 7–14 days to optimize. We recommend not making changes to the campaign in the first two weeks. Consistent results typically appear in weeks 3–4.
Q: Can I customize my email sequence?
A: Yes. Your email sequence is fully editable inside your GHL account. Go to Automation → Workflows → [Your Nurture Sequence]. You can edit the subject lines, body copy, and timing of each email. We recommend keeping the structure but personalizing the language to match your voice.
Q: What is a Thesis Presentation and how do I use it?
A: Your Thesis Presentation is a 12-slide deck that makes the logical argument for why a producing agent should join your brokerage. You use it in two ways: (1) as a leave-behind after a strategy call — send the PDF to agents who want to "think about it", and (2) as the foundation for your VSL script. The stronger your Thesis, the higher your conversion rate on calls.

Ongoing maintenance: Update the knowledge base whenever a new FAQ comes up in client support emails. If the same question is asked twice, it belongs in the knowledge base. The goal is for the AI bot to handle 80% of client questions without VA involvement.
